Step 2 of 5 · Nervous System Regulation

Physiological Sigh

Two physiological sighs — the fastest known way to down-regulate the sympathetic nervous system. Double-inhale fills collapsed alveoli; long exhale activates the vagal brake.

Press start
Developed by Huberman Lab, Stanford Neuroscience · 2023

Widen your gaze. Let your eyes soften and your peripheral vision expand. Unclench the jaw.

Panoramic vision shifts the visual cortex from narrow threat-focus to wide-field mode, reducing amygdala activation. Let the whole room come into your awareness.

Follow the dot with your eyes. Smooth bilateral movement activates the prefrontal cortex and down-regulates arousal. No effort — let the eyes move comfortably.

Smooth pursuit eye movement (SPEM) activates the cerebellum and reduces limbic arousal — the same mechanism underlying bilateral stimulation in EMDR.
